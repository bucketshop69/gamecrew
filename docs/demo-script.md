## Part 2 — The walkthrough script (your spoken part)

Speak it natural — these are beats, not lines to memorize. [SCREEN] notes say
what plays beside you while you talk. Target: a bit over three minutes.

**[SCREEN: hold on your face for the first two lines, then Home screen]**

What you just saw is GameCrew — and everything in that trailer is the real
app. Let me walk you through how it works, and more importantly, why I made
the choices behind it.

I built GameCrew as a mobile application first, because that's where football
already lives. Most fans watch a match with a phone in their hand — checking
the score, reading reactions, trying to understand what just happened. That
phone is the second screen. That's also why Solana Mobile is the natural
starting audience: a mobile-first community with a real distribution path.

**[SCREEN: Home screen, slow scroll through matches]**

Visually, I kept GameCrew almost entirely black and white — on purpose.
The app doesn't bring its own colors. Each match brings its identity —
the teams, the flags, the kits, the banners. GameCrew provides the stage;
football provides the color.

The home screen is deliberately simple: matches that matter now, upcoming
fixtures, completed games you can replay. No news feed, no dashboard between
the fan and the game.

**[SCREEN: open England vs Argentina — full-time board, then sound on]**

Open a match, and GameCrew becomes more than a score app. Everything you
see is built from the real match feed — every event, every update. Match
Pulse turns that into live commentary you can read. And because nobody
wants to stare at text, the match also speaks. Turn the sound on and
you've got a commentator in your pocket, calling the game over the crowd —
and everything he says comes from what actually happened.

**[SCREEN: Game View during a busy spell — players, LED boards]**

And if you'd rather watch than read — Game View draws the match for you,
live from those same events. The pressure building, the goals, the
celebrations. Read it, hear it, or watch it — it's always the same match,
the same moment. Even the LED boards around the pitch are part of the
plan: that's where clubs and partners will live, just like a real stadium.

**[SCREEN: tap the 55' checkpoint — goal clip plays, settles back]**

Missed the game? Every big moment is a checkpoint. Tap one and you get it
as a clip — the buildup, the goal, the call. Or tap Play highlights and
the whole match folds into a reel. You catch up in three minutes, like
television.

**[SCREEN: chat sheet — pinned calls, tap a pick, then the Board]**

Then comes the fun. Joining a match hands you a playful gift — a bike, a
house, even a Lambo. Use it to make a call on what happens next; the real
match settles it. Win calls, earn Coolness, climb the board — and claim
your best trophies as collectibles you can show off anywhere. No deposits,
no cash-outs. Just bragging rights.

**[SCREEN: leave a live match — the listening bar on Home]**

And on a live game, the commentary follows you out — like a podcast of the
match you care about. That's the idea in one line: GameCrew is a companion
fans keep open, not an app they check.

**[SCREEN: landing page flash, then the full-time board, hold]**

I built all of this solo for this hackathon — there's a landing page and
docs if you want to go deeper.

Pick a match. Follow the pulse. Watch the play. Bet your Lambo — playfully.

That's GameCrew — the match, in your hand.

## If judges ask (Q&A pocket — not part of the video)

- Voice: xAI text-to-speech ("Kepler" voice), generated offline; a full
  match costs ~20 cents; every line fingerprinted so only changed lines
  ever regenerate.
- Truth: TxLINE owns match facts; commentary is validated against them
  (no invented events, comeback/equaliser claims checked by rules).
- Game View: stylized probable play from semantic frames — deliberately
  not claiming video or player tracking.
- Collectibles: Solana devnet claims via embedded wallet; no real-money
  wagering anywhere.
- Works identically on live matches; finished matches are the bottled
  demo form.
